Yields

14 entries

Growth
When a City's Growth bar fills up, a new Citizen becomes available. Growth also determines how fast a City can produce Civilian Units, such as Settlers and Workers. While a City produces a Civilian Unit, its Growth bar no longer fills. Ways to improve a City's Growth rate include building Farms on crop resources, Nets on Fish or Crabs, and Granaries, as well as training Farmers and Fishers.
Civics
Cities produce Specialists and Projects with their Civics. Unused Civics go into the Global stockpile, which can be used to enact Laws, establish Theologies, construct Wonders, Hurry Production, perform Missions like Pacify City, and other uses. You can stockpile a maximum of Civics, with excess converted into Science. Increase Civics by training Stonecutters and establishing Forums I. Members of your Court with high Charisma also contribute to the global Civics stockpile. In the case of a Deficit, one or more Cities will automatically switch Production to a Council.
Training
Cities produce Military Units with their Training. Unused Training goes into the Global stockpile, which can be used to buy Orders, conduct a Forced March, assign Generals, and for Unit Promotions and Upgrades. You can stockpile a maximum of Training, with excess converted into Orders. Increase Training by training Miners and building Barracks or Ranges. Members of your Court with high Courage also contribute to the global Training stockpile. In the case of a Deficit, one or more Cities will automatically switch Production to a Council.
Culture
When a City's Culture bar fills up, a positive Event occurs, and the City's overall Culture Level increases. The four Culture Levels are Weak, Developing, Strong, and Legendary. Culture Level affects which Improvements and Wonders can be built in a City's territory and whether the City can Hurry Production, which requires at least Developing Culture. Cities with higher Culture levels also cost additional Food, Iron, Stone, and Wood per Population. Ways to increase a City's Culture yield include building Shrines and Odeons, training certain Specialists, spreading a Religion to a City, and adopting certain Laws, such as Epics.
Happiness
When a City's Happiness bar fills up, its overall Happiness Level goes up by one, which increases Growth and reduces Maintenance. Some ways to increase Happiness include connecting your City with the Trade Network, holding a Festival, adopting Laws such as Tolerance, keeping a City's Religions happy, and building Baths.
Discontent
When a City's Happiness bar empties, its overall Discontent Level goes up by one, which reduces Science and increases Maintenance. Higher Discontent Levels also reduce the Opinion of the City's ruling Family. Some ways to slow Discontent include connecting your City with the Trade Network, holding a Festival, adopting Laws such as Tolerance, keeping a City's Religions happy, and building Baths.
Science
Science increases the rate at which your Nation discovers new Technologies. Science yields can be increased by building Archives I and by training Specialists in your Cities. Laws such as Centralization and some religious Theologies can also help you generate more Science. Members of your Court with high Wisdom also contribute to Science yields.
Money
Money can be earned and spent by respectively selling and buying Food, Iron, Stone, and Wood. Money yields can be increased by building Hamlets and Markets, producing Treasuries in your Cities, and adopting Laws such as Tyranny. Members of your Court with high Discipline also contribute to Money yields. In the case of a Deficit, resources will be automatically sold to make up the difference.
Maintenance
Maintenance decreases the amount of Money a City generates. A City's Maintenance is increased by a higher Population, the City's distance from your Capital, and each Improvement within the City's borders. City Maintenance can be reduced by connecting the City to your Trade Network and improving the Opinion of the City's ruling Family.
Orders
Orders are needed for various Unit actions such as moving, attacking, and building Improvements. They are also needed for such actions as assigning Governors and sending Councilors on Missions. Unused Orders are sold for Money at the end of the Turn. Your Orders rate can be increased by improving your Legitimacy and building Garrisons.
Food
The best source of Food is usually Farms, which are most fruitful next to Fresh Water and on Lush Terrain. Food can also be acquired by building Nets and Pastures, and by training Farmers, Fishers, and Ranchers. Food is needed to feed your Population and to produce new Settlers. In the case of a Deficit, Money will be spent to buy the missing resource.
Iron
The primary source of Iron is Mines, which are most productive on Ore and on Hills. Iron is needed to build many types of Military Units, including Warriors and Axemen, which also require some Iron each turn as upkeep. In the case of a Deficit, Money will be spent to buy the missing resource.
Stone
The primary source of Stone is Quarries, which are most productive on Marble, on Arid Terrain, and adjacent to Mountains. Stone is needed primarily to build Improvements, such as Shrines, and Projects, such as Walls. In the case of a Deficit, Money will be spent to buy the missing resource.
Wood
Wood can be gathered either by chopping down Trees or by building Lumbermills once Forestry has been researched. Wood is necessary for building some City Improvements, such as Ranges, Military Units, such as Archers, and Ship Units, such as Biremes. These units also require some Wood each turn as upkeep. In the case of a Deficit, Money will be spent to buy the missing resource.
