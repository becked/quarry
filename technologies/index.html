Technologies

108 entries

Ironworking
This essential tech unlocks Warriors, early Melee Infantry that will form the backbone of your army until they can be upgraded after researching later techs.
Stonecutting
Stonecutting allows you to build Quarries in your Cities, an essential Improvement that provides a steady income of Stone. It also allows the construction of Forts to provide an early line of protection, increasing the defense, line of sight, and healing of a unit on the same Tile.
Stone Boost
This card will give you a one-off bonus of +200 Stone when researched, a useful boost for building Forts, Wonders, or other large projects. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Trapping
This early tech unlocks Slingers, a Ranged unit able to attack from a distance, and Camps, a diverse Improvement that provides essential Orders, when built on Camels or Elephants, or helps to improve your City with Growth or Culture when built on Game or Fur respectively.
Divination
Divination grants the ability to build various Shrines, one for each of your Nation's four pagan gods. The first Shrine built in your Territory will found your Nation's Pagan Religion, and each Shrine built will increase the Opinion of the Religion and its followers towards you. Characters and Families may convert to your Pagan Religion.
Administration
Administration allows for the improvement of your Cities through the construction of Granaries, which increase Growth and boost the Food output of adjacent Farms, and the Treasury I, an upgradeable Project that provides Cities with a steady income of Money.
Free Worker
This bonus card will grant you a free Worker at your Capital when researched, a useful way to develop the land around your Cities. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Labor Force
Labor Force allows your Workers to add Roads to Tiles. Although Roads can be slow and expensive to build, they are essential in expanding your Trade Network and keeping your Cities Connected and can also provide a great way to quickly move your Units to the front-line. Labor Force also opens the choice between the Freedom and Slavery Laws, and it is the path towards researching both Polearm and naval Units.
Husbandry
The humble-seeming Husbandry unlocks the building of Pastures on Tiles with grazing animals as a source of Growth and Food, or on Horses for Orders. Husbandry also leads to further Research that unlocks Mounted Units.
Food Boost
This bonus card will give you +200 Food when researched, a useful boost for supporting your armies and population. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Drama
Let there be music! In addition to starting the game's music, researching Drama unlocks the Odeon, an Improvement that adds to a City’s Culture yield to help it develop.
Free Settler
This bonus card will grant you a free Settler at your Capital when researched, a useful way to quickly expand your Nation. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Polis
Polis is useful for both defending and expanding your Cities. Building Walls increases the HP of a City and can increase Happiness if you keep a Unit from the same Family stationed in the walled City. Hamlets are Urban Improvements that can be built anywhere within your Territory. Over time they will develop into Villages and then Towns, consuming Food but providing increased yields of Money, especially when built on your Trade Network.
Military Drill
An essential step towards Unit Upgrades and Promotions, Military Drill unlocks the Rally Troops Mission for a one-time boost to your Training stockpile. Military Drill also allows for the construction of Barracks, which increase the Training output of your Cities and generate XP for any Military Unit on the same Tile. This ensures that your army grows stronger even during times of peace.
Aristocracy
Aristocracy is vital as it enables the appointment of an Ambassador, who can partake in diplomatic Missions to arrange a Peace or Truce, a Trade Mission, or even to Demand Tribute from another Nation. Aristocracy also unlocks the Centralization Law, which increases your production of Science and allows the improvement of Marsh Tiles, and the Vassalage Law, which reduces the burden of your army's upkeep if you are aiming to field a large force.
Border Boost
This bonus card will expand the borders in all of your Cities, granting you additional Territory. This is especially useful if there are resources just beyond reach. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Rhetoric
Rhetoric supports the early development of your Nation by allowing you to enact either the Epics Law, which grants Culture for each kill, or through Exploration, which allows your Scouts to travel across water. Rhetoric also allows for the Production of a Forum I in your Cities, a permanent and upgradable Project that grants a Civics yield for such things as training Specialists, enacting Laws, or performing Missions.
Navigation
The first step in naval research, Navigation encourages exploration of the seas by allowing the automation of your Scouts and by unlocking Biremes. The Colonies Law can help you expand your Cities through buying surrounding Tiles, while Serfdom grants additional Orders to your Nation each turn.
Free Bireme
This bonus grants a free Bireme naval Unit on a coastal Tile close to your Capital. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Phalanx
The Phalanx unlocks the Spearman, a Polearm Unit that is especially strong against Mounted Units, which are unable to move through a Polearm Unit’s ZOC. The Spearman's Pierce attack deals extra damage to a Unit directly behind the target of your attack, making the Spearman great both at pinning down mounted Units and holding the front line against a group of enemies.
Orders Boost
This bonus grants a one-off boost of +20 Orders on the turn it is completed, opening opportunities for a productive turn from both military and Worker Units. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Spoked Wheel
The Spoked Wheel tech is useful on multiple fronts. It allows you to appoint a Chancellor, who can aid in managing your Families and Cities through various Missions. It also unlocks the Chariot, the first Mounted Unit, in any City with Horses. These fast Units are good at flanking and have the Rout ability, allowing them to attack again if they kill an enemy Unit in combat.
Free Chariot
This bonus grants a free Chariot Unit in your Capital. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Forestry
The Forestry tech allows the construction of Lumbermills, which provide a steady source of Wood to support your ranged Units and free your Workers from needing to cut down trees.
Free Court Scholar
This bonus card grants a free Court Scholar to serve on your court and improve your Nation’s research efforts. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Steel
Steel unlocks the Axeman, which can be built in your Cities or upgraded from Warriors. These mid-tier melee combatants attack in an arc to hit multiple Tiles in front of them and do extra damage to Spearmen and other Polearm Units, making them very effective front-line fighters to support your Mounted troops against other Infantry Units.
Training Boost
This bonus gives a one-off boost of Training, useful for Forced Marching or Promoting your military Units. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Sovereignty
Sovereignty is an important step towards Siege Units and a useful tech for shaping your Nation. The laws of Tyranny and Constitution offer a choice between gaining resources by keeping military Units in your own Territory or by training more Specialists in your Cities.
Civics Boost
This bonus grants a one-time boost of +800 Civics, useful for enacting Laws, starting Missions, or building Wonders. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Metaphysics
Metaphysics can help you control the Religions in your Nation through various Missions. With Metaphysics researched, your Leader will be able to convince other Characters to Convert to the State Religion to keep your court united by faith, while the High Synod Mission sends an Ambassador to try to improve relations with a Religion.
Coinage
Coinage is a strong economic support tech that allows for the automation of Workers and unlocks the Market, an Improvement that boosts the Money income of the City. If your Nation has a Chancellor, they will be able to use the Pacify City Mission to reduce Discontent.
Money Boost
This bonus grants +4000 Money when researched, a useful boost for paying for Missions or trading for other resources. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Citizenship
The Citizenship tech can greatly enhance your Civics output through the construction of the Courthouse and through the Legal Code Law. Alternatively, the Divine Rule Law can help a religious Nation by allowing the adoption of your Pagan Religion as the State Religion.
Free Court Minister
This bonus card grants a free Court Minister to serve on your court and improve your Nation’s administration. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Portcullis
Portcullis is a vital technology that unlocks the appointment of a Spymaster, who can be sent on Missions to undermine your enemies through Infiltration or Assassination. It also allows for the construction of Moats to further defend your Cities with existing Walls.
Free Maceman
This bonus grants a free Maceman Infantry Unit in your Capital, useful against other Infantry. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Land Consolidation
Land Consolidation makes use of the various resources within your City Territories. Expensive but powerful and versatile Camel Archers and War Elephants can be built in Cities where Camels and Elephants are respectively available. Groves can provide further Culture to develop your City and can be improved with a Gardener Specialist to gain one of the Luxuries used to gain favor with Families, Cities, or other Nations.
Free Camel Archer

Free War Elephant

Composite Bow
The Composite Bow technology unlocks Archers, a mid-tier Ranged Unit with both improved damage and range over the Slinger. Once unlocked, it is also possible to upgrade your existing Slingers to Archers.
Free Archer
This bonus grants a free Archer Ranged Unit in your Capital, excellent as ranged support or for garrisoning Cities. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Monasticism
Monasticism encourages making good use of your Nation's Religion, as the Monotheism Law increases your Orders for each City that converts to your State Religion, while Polytheism allows the building of unlimited Pagan Shrines. The Monastery can be built by a Disciple of a World Religion to provide an income of Science.
Machinery
Machinery further develops the ranged capabilities of your armies. It unlocks the first of the Siege Units, the Onager, which deals Splash damage to Tiles adjacent to the target, although it needs to be Unlimbered before it can fire - a strong but slow Unit that will need protection. The Range allows a Ranged Unit on the same tile to gain XP when idle, allowing your forces to improve even during times of peace.
Free Onager
This bonus grants a free Onager Siege Unit in your Capital. If a bonus card is not researched when it appears, it is permanently trashed once discarded and will not appear again.
Scholarship
A gateway to further economic and civic developments, Scholarship allows the construction of Libraries, Academies, and Universities to improve your rate of research through Science.
Free Court Scholar

Stirrups

Architecture
The study of Architecture allows the construction of Cold Baths, Warm Baths, and Heated Baths for increasing Happiness in your Cities. It also opens Laws that help construction: Philosophy speeds up the recruitment of Specialists, while Engineering aids in building Wonders and Improvements.
Manor
Manor is a useful tech for improving your military. Conscripts are an upgrade to the base Militia Unit and are Polearm Units with a bonus against Mounted Units. This makes them an effective and cheap option for stopping flanking cavalry or defending your Cities. The choice will also open up between the Professional Army or Volunteers Laws, which provide alternative ways to increase your Nation’s Training.
Goods Boost

Battle Line
Researching Battle Line will unlock the Maceman Unit. An upgrade from the Axeman, Macemen lose the arcing Cleave attack, but their damage bonus is against all Infantry rather than just Polearm Units.
Free Court Soldier

Doctrine
Further developing your Nation’s Religions, Doctrine opens the decision between Tolerance, which increases Happiness for each Religion practiced in your Cities, and Orthodoxy, which lets you purge unwanted Religions to reduce the threat of religious division. It also unlocks Temples, which provide Culture for developing your Cities.
Hydraulics
Hydraulics support your Cities by allowing the construction of Watermills and Windmills, which provide a steady income of Science and greatly improve the output of adjacent Mines, Quarries, and Lumbermills. Hydraulics also unlocks the Ballista, an anti-Infantry Siege weapon. Although slow and short-ranged, the Ballista's deals Pierce damage that can hit two further enemies in a line behind the target at reduced damage.
Free Ballista

Cartography
Researching Cartography will aid in your dominance of the sea through the construction of the mid-tier Trireme naval Unit and the building of Harbors, which can train idle naval Units on the same Tile to provide XP and provide a steady income of Money. A Nation with a Spymaster will also be able to attempt to Steal Research from other Nations, a risky but effective way to pull ahead in the tech race.
Lateen Sail
The Lateen Sail marks the pinnacle of ship-building by unlocking the Dromon, a powerful naval Unit. It also opens two strong economic Laws. The Autarky Law aids your self-sufficiency by increasing your resource collection and allows the hiring of Units that normally need Horses, Camels, or Elephants anywhere. Alternatively, the Trade League Law promotes the Growth of your Cities and increases the income acquired from selling resources.
Jurisprudence
Jurisprudence allows you to use Discontent against your enemy by having an Agent perform an Insurrection Mission in an enemy City, or by reducing the Discontent in your own Cities through the Guilds Law. Alternatively, the Elites Law is useful for managing Orders, both providing more each turn and allowing you to carry over any unused Orders between turns.

Vaulting
