DYNASTY_PTOLEMY
Ptolemy I Soter heads the science-oriented Ptolemaic dynasty. Ptolemy can recruit Court Scholars and build the Shrine of Serapis.
DYNASTY_LEONIDAS
Spartan King Leonidas is a uniquely powerful warrior who can greatly accelerate early expansion. He will however not live long and it will be up to his successors to secure their father's legacy.
DYNASTY_PERICLES
The Athenian statesman Pericles is a very versatile leader, a patron of arts as skilled at nation-building as he is at warfare. Whether Pericles focuses on governance or battlefield leaderships, he will excel at the task.
DYNASTY_ALEXANDER
Young Alexander leads Greece shortly after his father's death. In addition to being a powerful warrior, Alexander is helped by people like the great philosopher Aristotle and others that will join the court. Alexander's mother, the scheming Queen Olympias, is uniquely capable politically and can Influence Olympias other Characters on the Leader's behalf.
Amanirenas
The Warrior Queen who led Kush against the Romans, attacking Roman-occupied Egypt and then defending Kush until Rome agreed to peace.
Amanitore
Amanitore is an unsurpassed builder, able to lead Kush into an age of prosperity. Her bonus orders mean that there is always time to be building.
Piye
The zealous Piye, the Pharaoh who conquered Egypt, leads Kush in a holy war under the banner of the god Amani (known in Egypt as Amun). He gains new troops for spreading a pagan religion.
Alara
Alara is the revered founder of Nepatan Kush, who united the tribes together into a nation. As Leader, Cities founded under Alara start with a bonus to Culture.
Shanakdakhete
Shanakdakhete is the first of the Queens of Kush, who helped to turn Meroë into a capital of culture and trade. She starts with the Literature resource and gets access to Caravans through meeting new Nations.
Kaleb
Kaleb is a renowned champion of Christianity who helped expand the territory, trade network, and influence of the kingdom of Aksum.
Ezana
Ezana is known for converting Aksum to Christianity. As an Evangelist, all Infantry Units produced in a City where he is Governor are Adept and follow Christianity, allowing them to Spread Religion.
Gudit
Gudit burned down her nation's churches and monuments as revenge for the church selling her into slavery. Though Infamous, she can Intimidate her detractors into becoming Terrified of her.
Makeda
Known in legend as the Queen of Sheba, Makeda is able to perform the unique Lead Delegation Mission, which leads to mutually beneficial outcomes with her neighbors.
Gadarat
One of the earliest rulers of Aksum, Gadarat expanded his nation from a mere city state to a mighty empire. A Worker is granted for each Tribal Site cleared.
Shammuramat
Queen Shammuramat starts the game as a Hero, but can change into a Builder and back again, allowing her to excel at both aspects of the game.
Tukulti-Ninurta I
Tukulti-Ninurta I gains considerable personal Legitimacy by declaring war on other Nations, which makes him uniquely suited to early conflict.
Ashur-Uballit I
Ashur-Ubalit I automatically gains favors from the first Nation contacted, allowing him to ruthlessly manipulate early diplomacy.
Sargon II
Sargon II can enact Epics from the start of the game, allowing the building of early Garrisons and rapid Culture gains through early fighting.
Adad-Guppi
Adad-Guppi is an Intelligent Queen, with a uniquely long lifespan.
Hammurabi
Hammurabi has access to the unique Court of the Divine King Mission, which replaces Hold Court, produces double the output, and has twice the chance of recruiting courtiers.
Nebuchadnezzar I
Under Nebuchadnezzar's rule, ranged units are hidden on forests, and all units gain increased vision on the map. Upon conquering his first Barbarian or Tribal camp, Nebuchadnezzar I will receive an extra Slinger and an extra Scout.
Kurigalzu I
Under Kurigalzu's rule, Babylonia can buy Tiles with Training, allowing the redirection of military power toward peaceful territorial expansion.
DYNASTY_HANNIBAL
As a General, Hannibal enables his unit to take two actions per turn, such as attacking twice, or healing and attacking. At just 26 years of age, Hannibal can have several decades in which to lead Carthage to glory.
Hanno II
As a Leader, Hanno II can issue Land Grants to boost Family Opinions. The talented Hannibal is on Hanno's court, although the two men have significant disagreements. Hanno is a strong advocate of dominating weaker Tribes and will go to war with the first Tribe Carthage encounters.
Hanno the Navigator
As a Leader, Hanno the Navigator will gain access to a unique Explorer early in his rule, and will be able to offer a Tribal Alliance to one Tribe. Hanno the Navigator lacks the political power of earlier monarchs and has a weaker relationship with the Families.
Merneith
As a General, Merneith's adjacent units will enjoy an attack bonus into Connected Tiles.
Khufu
Khufu is a great administrator of monumental works and is able to build Wonders for less cost. If Khufu builds The Pyramids, then that City will receive a boost after his death.
Mentuhotep II
Mentuhotep II is able to perform the Missions Family Gift Mentuhotep Ii, Pacify City Mentuhotep Ii, and Imprison Mentuhotep Ii immediately, without the need for a Chancellor.
Akhenaten
Akhenaten's Religious Fervor has led to his insanity, but his unique Pagan Religion Atenism will lead to lower Discontent in Cities following the State Religion.
Ramesses II
Ramesses II inspires his people as the Great Ancestor, and conquers his enemies with Chariot Master.
Suppiluliuma I
Suppiluliuma, and his military, gain additional XP automatically.
Hantili I
As Hantili the Stalwart Defender, all newly founded Cities will start with Walls.
Puduhepa
Queen Puduhepa enables shrines to be built from the beginning of the game, and her Syncretism further leverages Pagan Religion.
Mursili II
Although Mursili II starts with a Legitimacy penalty, he and his companion Nuvanza are able to soon make up for it by choosing an early ambition.
DYNASTY_DARIUS
Darius is an effective reformer of national governance, with the ability to appoint Governors at half the Civics cost. He also has a problem in the face of Bardiya, a briefly reigning King whom Darius deposed and who still has designs on the throne.
DYNASTY_SHAPUR
Shapur is an aggressive conqueror whose Melee and Ship Units start with Strike I. He gets access to a unique Found Religion Project and, if Zoroastrianism becomes the State Religion, Shapur will receive favorable events from building a positive relationship with the Religion.
DYNASTY_STATEIRA
Stateira is a queen with ambitious building projects. She can press citizens into service to obtain additional Militias. Stateira and her husband have many enemies within the court, so the queen will have to deal with internal power struggles and threats from her mother-in-law and brother-in-law.
DYNASTY_CYAXARES
Huvaxštra, or Cyaxares, is a military leader who achieves victory through a higher quality of units. He is especially capable at fighting tribal opponents.
Scipio
Scipio is a masterful tactician, able to stay hidden from shrubs and trees as a General. His dynasty also gets a Happiness bonus from killing units of other Nations.
Sulla
Sulla rules Rome with an iron fist. His unique Dictator Trait means that Family Opinion will get steadily worse over time, although he has the Proscription Mission to keep any unruly families in line.
Marius
Marius' reforms to the Roman military mean that the loyal units of his dynasty get a bonus when led by a General. He also has a unique Trait that allows the building of Enrollment Posts, Projects that speed up the training of new Warrior Units.
Julius Caesar
Caesar is an early expansionist. All units gain Training for killing Tribal units and start with bonus XP. This allows Caesar to quickly promote Units. He also starts with some strong Generals, although you should beware the Ides of March.
Augustus
Augustus, the first Emperor of Rome, is concerned with maintaining his legitimacy and a stable dynasty. Leaders from his dynasty can use the Adopt as Heir Mission, which lets them adopt any Royal younger than them as the heir without the normal legitimacy cost for choosing an heir.
Agrippina
Agrippina rules Rome with cunning and guile. Able to appoint a Spymaster from the start of the game, she is adept at keeping both enemies and allies in line by any means. This is especially useful given the threat of her unpredictable heir Nero...
